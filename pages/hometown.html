City of Heroes

Surabaya, the capital of East Java, is often referred to as the "City of Heroes" due to its significant role in Indonesia's struggle for independence. Known for its vibrant culture and bustling economy, Surabaya is the second-largest city in Indonesia and serves as a major trading and shipping hub. The city boasts a blend of modern skyscrapers and historical landmarks, reflecting its rich cultural heritage. Surabaya's culinary scene is also renowned, offering a variety of delicious local dishes that attract food enthusiasts. With its dynamic atmosphere and diverse population, Surabaya stands as a symbol of resilience and progress in Indonesia. Additionally, the city hosts various cultural events and festivals throughout the year, celebrating its unique traditions and fostering a sense of community among its residents.

Geographic Location

Geographically, Surabaya is situated on the northern coast of Java, specifically in East Java Province. The city lies between 7°9' and 7°21' South Latitude and 112°36' to 112°54' East Longitude. It borders the Madura Strait to the north and east, Sidoarjo Regency to the south, and Gresik Regency to the west.

Covering an area of 326.81 square kilometers, Surabaya primarily consists of lowland terrain, with elevations ranging from 3 to 6 meters above sea level. The southern part features two gentle hills in the Lidah and Gayungan areas, rising between 25 and 50 meters above sea level. Additionally, Surabaya is home to the estuary of the Kalimas River, one of the two branches of the Brantas River, enhancing its geographical significance as a thriving urban center.

History

Surabaya's rich history predates the colonial era, with the Trowulan I inscription from 1358 AD indicating its original name, Śūrabhaya, a vital village along the Brantas River. Scholars suggest the city was established in 1275 AD by King Kertanegara for his soldiers. A captivating legend recounts the fierce battle between Adipati Jayengrono and Sawunggaling; after defeating the Mongol forces, Raden Wijaya founded a palace in Ujung Galuh and appointed Jayengrono as leader. However, Jayengrono's crocodile magic threatened the Majapahit Kingdom, leading to a legendary seven-day duel with Sawunggaling along the Kali Mas in Peneleh. By the 14th century, the name Śūrabhaya was officially recognized, solidifying the city's significance.

Current Leaders

Eri Cahyadi and his deputy Armuji, who began leading Surabaya in February 2021, have made remarkable strides in improving the city. Under their leadership, significant infrastructure development has taken place, including the construction of over 584 kilometers of roads and 1,177 neighborhood halls (Balai RW) for public services. They have also expanded public lighting by installing 105,846 streetlights across thousands of locations. To address seasonal flooding, the administration has built over 109 kilometers of drainage channels and seven new pumping stations, bringing the total to 75 pumps by 2023. The city has also prioritized the creation of green spaces, revitalizing 921 parks and expanding public green areas to over 7,300 hectares, surpassing national standards.
