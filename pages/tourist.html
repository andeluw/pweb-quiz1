Tugu Pahlawan

Tugu Pahlawan is a monumental landmark in Surabaya, standing 41.15 meters tall with a unique inverted nail shape. Featuring 10 curved sections symbolizing the significant date of November 10, 1945, it commemorates the Battle of Surabaya, where the city's brave residents fought against Allied and Dutch forces seeking to re-colonize Indonesia after World War II. This pivotal battle showcased the courage of the Surabaya people, known as arek-arek Suroboyo.

Located on Jalan Pahlawan near the East Java Governor's Office, Tugu Pahlawan is an iconic symbol of Surabaya, known as the "City of Heroes." Surrounded by landscaped gardens, the 1.3-hectare area attracts both locals and tourists, providing a serene environment for reflection. Every November 10, the site becomes the focal point for commemorations, drawing thousands to honor the heroes who sacrificed their lives for the nation's freedom. Tugu Pahlawan serves as a reminder of Surabaya's pivotal role in Indonesia's independence and its legacy as a center of national pride.

Kebun Binatang Surabaya

Kebun Binatang Surabaya (KBS), also known as Surabaya Zoo, is one of Indonesia's most popular zoos, located in Surabaya. Once regarded as the most comprehensive zoo in Southeast Asia, KBS is home to over 230 species and more than 2,179 animals, including rare species from Indonesia and around the world across various categories such as mammals, birds, reptiles, and fish. Visitors can enjoy a variety of attractions, including opportunities to ride animals like elephants and camels, take photos with birds and reptiles, explore a kids' zoo, and experience boat tours around the proboscis monkey island. The zoo also features animal edutainment programs and the unique Aquanoctudio, making it a vibrant destination for conservation, education, and recreation.

Monumen Kapal Selam

Monumen Kapal Selam, or Monkasel, is a unique submarine museum located in Embong Kaliasin, Genteng, Surabaya. It features the KRI Pasopati 410, a Soviet-made submarine from 1952 that played a role in Indonesia's military history, particularly during the Battle of Aru to liberate Irian Barat from Dutch occupation. The submarine has been transformed into a museum, allowing visitors to explore its interior while also learning about its historical significance through film screenings and guided tours. Surrounded by amenities such as restaurants and souvenir shops, Monkasel offers an educational experience, making it a notable attraction in Surabaya. The museum highlights Indonesia's naval heritage and the challenges faced by sailors, providing insight into the country's maritime history and military achievements. Additionally, Monkasel serves as a symbol of national pride, commemorating the sacrifices made by Indonesian sailors in their pursuit of independence. Visitors can enjoy a scenic view of the surrounding area, enhancing their experience at this historic site.

Kya Kya

Kya-Kya in Jalan Kembang Jepun, Surabaya, is a revitalized Chinatown area that has become a notable tourist destination. Officially reopened on September 10, 2022, the site aims to serve as a new cultural and culinary attraction. Visitors can explore the historic neighborhood on traditional becaks, visit ancient temples, and taste a variety of Chinese-Indonesian street foods. The area features around 60 local vendors, including 30 from the surrounding community, offering food and goods. Kya-Kya operates on weekends, with plans for further development to enhance its appeal. The local government has introduced initiatives such as repainting buildings in symbolic colors—black, red, and gold—and installing bilingual nameplates in Chinese and Indonesian to preserve its cultural heritage. This development is part of a broader plan to establish Kya-Kya as a key cultural and economic hub in Surabaya. It serves as a platform for local micro, small, and medium enterprises (UMKM) to thrive, boosting the city's economy. As visitors immerse themselves in the vibrant atmosphere, they can engage with local artisans and participate in cultural events.
