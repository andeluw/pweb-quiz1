City of Heroes

Surabaya, the capital of East Java, is known as the "City of Heroes" for its pivotal role in Indonesia's fight for independence. A vibrant cultural and economic hub, it is Indonesia's second-largest city and a key center for trade and shipping. The city combines modern skyscrapers with historical landmarks, showcasing its rich heritage. Its culinary scene is renowned, attracting food enthusiasts from near and far. With a dynamic atmosphere and diverse population, Surabaya symbolizes resilience and progress. The city also continues to grow as a hub for education and technological innovation, drawing students and professionals alike. Additionally, Surabaya offers a well-developed infrastructure, making it a thriving urban destination for both residents and visitors.

Geographic Location

Geographically, Surabaya is situated on the northern coast of Java in East Java Province, between 7°9' to 7°21' South Latitude and 112°36' to 112°54' East Longitude. It borders the Madura Strait to the north and east, Sidoarjo Regency to the south, and Gresik Regency to the west. Covering 326.81 square kilometers, Surabaya primarily consists of lowland terrain, with elevations ranging from 3 to 6 meters above sea level. The southern part features two gentle hills, rising between 25 and 50 meters, and the Kalimas River estuary enhances its geographical significance as a thriving urban center. Its coastal location has historically made it a hub for trade and maritime activities. The city also experiences a tropical climate, with wet and dry seasons that shape its urban planning and infrastructure development.

History

Surabaya's history predates the colonial era, with the 1358 Trowulan I inscription marking its original name, Śūrabhaya, a vital village along the Brantas River. Scholars believe the city was founded in 1275 AD by King Kertanegara for his soldiers. After defeating the Mongol forces, Raden Wijaya founded a palace in Ujung Galuh and appointed Jayengrono as leader. Jayengrono's crocodile magic threatened the Majapahit Kingdom, leading to a legendary seven-day duel with Sawunggaling along the Kali Mas in Peneleh. By the 14th century, the name Śūrabhaya was officially recognized.

Current Leaders

Eri Cahyadi and his deputy Armuji, who began leading Surabaya in February 2021, have made remarkable strides in improving the city. Significant infrastructure development includes over 584 kilometers of roads and 1,177 neighborhood halls (Balai RW) for public services. They have also expanded public lighting by installing 105,846 streetlights across thousands of locations. To address seasonal flooding, the administration has built over 109 kilometers of drainage channels and added seven new pumping stations, bringing the total to 75 by 2023. The city also revitalized 921 parks and expanded green areas to over 7,300 hectares, surpassing national standards.
